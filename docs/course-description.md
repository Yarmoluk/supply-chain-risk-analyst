# Course Description

## Supply Chain Risk Management: ISO 28000 Framework

### Overview

This comprehensive course provides supply chain professionals with the knowledge and skills necessary to identify, assess, and mitigate risks across global supply chain operations. Built on the ISO 28000 security management framework, this course equips learners with evidence-based strategies for building resilient, secure, and efficient supply chains in an increasingly volatile business environment.

### Target Audience

This course is designed for:

- **Supply Chain Managers** responsible for end-to-end supply chain operations and risk oversight
- **Risk Analysts** focused on enterprise risk management and operational resilience
- **Procurement Professionals** managing supplier relationships and sourcing strategies
- **Logistics Coordinators** overseeing transportation, warehousing, and distribution networks
- **Operations Directors** seeking to enhance organizational resilience
- **Compliance Officers** implementing ISO 28000 certification programs
- **Business Continuity Planners** developing crisis response capabilities

### Prerequisites

Students should have:

- Basic understanding of supply chain management principles
- Familiarity with business operations and organizational structures
- Foundational knowledge of risk management concepts (recommended but not required)
- No specific technical background required

### Course Topics

The course is structured around seven core topics:

1. **Supply Chain Risk Foundations** - Understanding risk categories, frameworks, and historical disruptions
2. **Risk Identification** - Methods and tools for discovering vulnerabilities in supply chains
3. **ISO 28000 Framework** - Implementing security management systems aligned with international standards
4. **Supplier Risk Management** - Evaluating, monitoring, and mitigating supplier-related risks
5. **Logistics and Disruption** - Managing transportation, inventory, and geographic risks
6. **Technology and Visibility** - Leveraging digital tools for real-time risk monitoring
7. **Resilience and Continuity** - Building adaptive capacity and crisis response capabilities

### Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, learners will be able to:

**Remember & Understand:**
- Define key supply chain risk categories and terminology
- Explain the structure and purpose of ISO 28000
- Describe common supply chain disruption scenarios

**Apply:**
- Conduct risk assessments using FMEA and risk matrices
- Implement supplier evaluation scorecards
- Apply PDCA cycle methodology to security management

**Analyze:**
- Evaluate single-source vs multi-source supplier strategies
- Assess supplier financial health and operational capacity
- Compare nearshoring, offshoring, and reshoring trade-offs

**Evaluate:**
- Critique supply chain resilience using industry metrics
- Judge the effectiveness of risk mitigation strategies
- Determine appropriate inventory buffering levels

**Create:**
- Design business continuity plans for supply chain disruptions
- Develop comprehensive supplier risk management programs
- Build integrated risk monitoring dashboards using modern tools

### Course Format

This intelligent textbook combines theoretical foundations with practical applications. Each chapter includes:

- Real-world case studies from recent supply chain disruptions
- Interactive diagrams and visual models
- Step-by-step implementation guides
- Self-assessment questions aligned with Bloom's taxonomy
- References to ISO standards and industry best practices

### Why This Course Matters

Recent global events have exposed critical vulnerabilities in supply chains worldwide. From the COVID-19 pandemic to semiconductor shortages and geopolitical tensions, organizations face unprecedented complexity and risk. This course provides the frameworks and tools necessary to navigate this challenging environment, protect business continuity, and build competitive advantage through supply chain resilience.

Whether you're implementing ISO 28000 certification, responding to board-level risk questions, or redesigning your organization's sourcing strategy, this course delivers actionable insights grounded in international standards and proven methodologies.
